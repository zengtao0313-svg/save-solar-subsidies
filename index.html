Why Solar Subsidies Matter

Solar subsidies help homeowners reduce upfront costs and accelerate clean energy adoption in Florida. Removing or reducing these incentives will slow progress and increase long-term energy costs for families.

Florida currently relies heavily on natural gas for electricity. Without solar incentives, residents become more vulnerable to rising fuel prices and utility rate hikes. Solar reduces this dependence, increases grid resilience, and keeps more money in local communities instead of going to energy corporations.

Solar growth also creates thousands of jobs across the state—from installation and engineering to sales and small-business growth. Reducing subsidies would shrink this job market and slow down Florida’s clean energy innovation.

Impact on Minority & Low-Income Communities

Solar subsidies are especially important for minority and low-income households, who historically carry a heavier energy burden. In Florida, many families living in disadvantaged areas spend a higher percentage of their income on electricity compared to wealthier neighborhoods.

Subsidies, rebates, and net-metering policies help close this gap by making clean energy financially accessible. When subsidies are removed or weakened:

  • Upfront solar installation becomes unaffordable for low-income families
  • Energy-burdened communities stay trapped in high utility bills
  • Minority communities miss out on long-term savings and energy independence
  • Local solar jobs—which often hire from underserved neighborhoods—decline

Keeping subsidies ensures that the shift to clean energy is equitable. Without support policies, wealthier households will continue to benefit while disadvantaged communities fall further behind.
