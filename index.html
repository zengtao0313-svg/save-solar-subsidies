Save Solar Subsidies in Florida

Florida is ending subsidies for solar panels that help small businesses and low-income residents. These programs are essential for promoting clean energy, lowering energy bills, and creating local green jobs. We urge the government to extend these programs to ensure solar energy is accessible to all Floridians.

Sign the Petition

Why It Matters

  • Reduce carbon emissions and move Florida toward a cleaner, more resilient energy future
  • Lower energy bills for small businesses, nonprofits, and working families
  • Help low-income communities access renewable energy that was previously unaffordable
  • Protect residents from rising utility costs caused by fossil fuel price volatility
  • Create high-paying local clean-energy jobs in installation and manufacturing
  • Boost resilience during hurricanes with rooftop solar + battery storage
  • Keep Florida competitive with other states accelerating renewable adoption
  • Ensure federal energy programs reach Florida families instead of going unused

What’s Happening in Florida

Florida’s solar future is uncertain. Recent policy changes at the federal and state level have reduced or eliminated key incentives that helped homeowners, condo residents, and small businesses install solar.

  • $156 million Solar for All grant canceled, removing support for low-income households
  • Federal tax credits may be weakened or allowed to expire in 2025
  • Utilities have pushed to weaken net-metering payments for solar owners
  • Florida offers fewer state-level solar incentives compared to other states
  • Multifamily housing and small businesses face higher upfront costs with no support

Without action, these changes will harm vulnerable communities and slow Florida’s progress toward a clean energy future.

Who Is Most Affected?

  • Low-income families who qualified for the Solar for All program
  • Renters and condo owners in buildings with shared rooftops
  • Small businesses trying to manage rising energy costs
  • Rural communities with limited access to affordable energy
  • Schools and community centers that rely on solar to reduce electric bills
